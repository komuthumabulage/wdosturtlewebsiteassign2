Species of Turtles

Sri Lanka is home to a diverse and robust population of turtles, which transform the island's coastal ecosystems as graceful nesters, coral reef residents, and sea giants. These creatures symbolize marine life's wonders and are a testament to the natural beauty and balance of this island paradise.

Sea Turtle Varieties in Sri Lanka

GREEN TURTLE

Green turtles are one of the largest sea turtle species found in Sri Lanka, measuring 3 to 4 feet in length and weighing between 90 to 225 kg. They exhibit a distinct greenish color and rely on seagrasses and algae for their diet. Female green turtles lay approximately 100 to 200 eggs per nest, which take 55 to 70 days to incubate.

OLIVE RIDLEY

Olive Ridley turtles are a medium-sized sea turtle species found in Sri Lanka. They have a heart-shaped olive green carapace, reaching 2 feet in length and weighing 80 to 100 pounds. They exhibit unique nesting behavior called arribadas, where thousands of females come ashore together to lay eggs.

LOGGERHEAD

The Loggerhead turtles are a unique species found in Sri Lanka, known for their large heads, strong jaws, and reddish-brown carapace.Loggerhead turtles grow to be around 3 to 4 feet in length and weigh approximately 200 to 350 pounds. They have an incubation period of 50-60 days, after that the hatchlings emerge and make their way to the sea.

HAWKSBILL

Hawksbill turtles are medium-sized sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, known for their distinct physical features, including a pointed beak and a beautifully patterned carapace. They feed on sponges, algae, and other marine invertebrates, and lay clutches of 100-150 eggs which take 60-70 days to hatch.

LEATHERBACK

The Leatherback turtle is a unique species found in Sri Lanka, with a distinct feeding style of consuming jellyfish and a leathery carapace with ridges. They can reach impressive sizes, up to 6-7 feet in length and weigh around 1,000-2,000 pounds. Their nesting behavior involves synchronized nesting and the incubation typically lasts 60-70 days before hatchlings emerge.
