ABOUT US

Varieties of Sea Turtles in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka has ten different turtle species, including five sea turtles and five freshwater turtles. These turtles are crucial for maintaining the biological balance of Sri Lanka's marine ecosystems and are also regarded as cultural treasures by the Sri Lankan people. These wonderful species must be protected to ensure their survival and to preserve Sri Lanka's natural beauty.

Threats to Turtles

Turtles in Sri Lanka are facing a range of threats, including pollution, poaching, habitat destruction, and overfishing. Sea turtles are particularly vulnerable to these threats, while freshwater turtles are at risk due to dam development and waterway contamination. To protect these creatures and maintain the ecological balance of Sri Lanka's natural resources, measures must be taken.

Turtle Hatcheries in Sri Lanka

Turtle incubation centers in Sri Lanka provide a safe haven for sea turtles and educate the local community about conservation efforts. Visitors can experience the enchantment of watching baby turtles hatch and make their way to the ocean. Supporting these hatcheries can help ensure the survival of Sri Lanka's turtles.
